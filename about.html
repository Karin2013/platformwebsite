This Project

Artificial light takes up huge amounts of space in our life. We use it to stay up all night to study, have dinner with friends in the restaurant, or even enjoy night life in the club. However, even though it takes up huge amounts of space in our life, it doesnâ€™t mean that it can replace natural light.

Sunlight uplifts our spirit. We sense a deep and strong relationship with nature. And it brings out the maximum capability of oneself when we start our day watching the sunrise. Also in architecture people live in, gently framed natural light visits our space, it curves sunlight. And from there, sunlight approaches us with a whole new different meaning from what we experience outside, containing or even extending narratives of space.

In the overpopulated city, however, we can no longer take natural light for granted in personal space. There is limited space in the city and sunlight has to be shared. In this project, we find the new meaning of light in our everyday life in the city to discover how owning light represents another form of power structure.
